MDDN342

The goal of this assignment was to create a system that maps attributes of the face, and expresses the extrinsic and intrinsic values using machine learning. It will act as a sort of filter, and use real world data in real time to project onto a “photobooth” type of application, using the webcam.

When I started this assignment I had one idea in my mind, and that was this sketching guide. I saw the opportunity to try and make something that is actually useful to someone, and something even I could use.

I ended up being a bit too pedantic and ambitious with how I wanted it to look, and it ended up causing me to fall behind. I had wanted to originally make it look like an actual sketched face, and be quick and messy looking. I soon realised that purposefully trying to make something look imperfect on purpose within this code space would be very very difficult. I knew that I was running out of time with this idea, and I didn’t want to finish this idea and leave it half done, so I ended up making a quick adjustment and turning it into a clown filter. I did this as a social commentary on the New Zealand police, and the different colours of the different parts of the face represented different values, like skin colour, hair colour, if they were smiling or not, etc.

After I had finished with my uni work for the year, I decided I wanted to try and fix it up to fit more inline with my first vision. I tried to simplify the idea, and think about what would actually be important. It ended up coming down to the face structure, so I had decided to just put baselines down. I drew up a sketch on how I wanted it to look, how I thought it would be helpful. It ended up looking slightly scary to me, but I actually quite liked it.

After analysing my sketch a bit, I discovered that I really liked the harsher and more straight lines. The stylised angles really caught me, so I spent some time trying to figure out how I would adjust my previous work to my new and improved idea. I had to swap out my curveVertexes to regular vertexes, and I had to map out very specific points. I used a function given to me by a lecturer to find the average point between two of the already existing points. This ended up being the golden answer, and really made my design pop. I then changed the AI to map the colour of the models skin, and change the colour of the guidelines.

Red = Dark

Yellow = Medium

Cyan = Tan

Green = Pale

Overall, compared to how I first did this, I am very happy with this outcome. I really think that this is a very helpful tool for people, and definitely for me. I would really like to develop this a lot further eventually, and make it very advanced at tracking landmarks of the face, and maybe think about involving it in AR.

INDN332

For this class, the goal was to create a product we would want to see implemented in the future to help society. This assignment pushed for us to create and dream of things that could be beneficial to society, and to start thinking about how we could design around that.

For my product, I designed an external patch for using drugs safely and securely. Named “DrugPlug”, it is designed to help people get the perfect high, perfectly tailored to them. It would work via a needle that will puncture the skin to take a sample of their biology. This will then be sent into the tiny microcomputer built within the dock of the patch, and be put through an AI algorithm to read and scan through everything. Once it has been through the AI, it will calculate a perfect dosage based on what the user has selected on the app. It will then take a few minutes to start preparing to send the user into the trip, and allows time for the user to set up.

I originally came up with this idea by thinking about what I thought was an issue with current society, and what would make people happier, healthier, and safer. In my ideal future society, a lot of the labour intensive jobs would be taken over, and society would instead have a huge focus around science and technology. Learning and recreation would be encouraged. I thought about how people around my age enjoy the occasional substance at a party, and how I could make it safer. I went through many versions of how this could work. At first it would be a little pocket device, and then I took inspiration from a brooch and decided to make it a removable patch

I decided to design this product because in an ideal future I would imagine that recreational drugs aren’t criminalised, and people are free to use them to have a good time and relax. I would also really want for people to be able to administer them safely, and not have to worry about an accidental overdose, or not getting the high they wanted. With the AI embedded in the technology, it would also help to prevent issues such as drug induced schizophrenia, addiction, psychotic episodes etc. Every Time the device is wanting to be used it will be reading the users biology and chemistry, and keep it monitored to help with these issues.

MDDN344

In this class, the goal was to use Unreal Engine for spatial visualisation, to display some of our past work and projects. This assignment worked as an interactive portfolio, to showcase some of our work and also push us to code some inputs.

For my assignment, I designed an eccentric artists workshop. I chose to do it like this because I found that most of my work varies a lot, and it is not very cohesive. I wanted to further drive this point home by making the workshop quite messy and a bit all over the place. I had random objects everywhere, some making lots of sense, like easels, and some getting quite unsettling, like dentistry tools. In the end I wanted it to emulate some of my fellow design students' lockers or desks, full of questionable but helpful things.

I showed some art that I had created outside of university, and two of my university projects, including a Coding project, and a Costume design report. In total, I had about 5 pieces being showcased. I chose to include my drawings because I felt it added more to the workshop scene, and made it seem more realistic. I added the coding project more as a side piece, and not a main because I had a computer screen, and I wanted it to seem like a screensaver, more leaning into that actual assignment. I added the costume design report as well, as I thought it seemed quite out there, but also it fit because of that reason. It didn’t make a lot of sense to actually be there, so I put it in, to help capture the Chaos.

INDN212

This course was about investigating how industrially designed products will respond to the people and environment around it. We were tasked with finding ideas for a responsive product, and then experiment and develop until we were left with a sleek and well thought out final product.

My idea revolved around an artistic experience. I made my product as a light installation, and it responded to voices and talking. The meaning behind it was meant to be a legitimate “conversation piece”, where talking would actually make it work. I wanted the experience to be about curiosity, wonder, and excitement. The Ideal interaction for this piece is some passing people, having a normal conversation while on a walk, and then all of a sudden, out of the corner of their eyes, they see something light up. They then walk over to investigate, and ask each other what it could be, when they realise, it actually is reacting to them talking! They then are excited about it, and play around with it, shouting, whispering, testing its capabilities. After this, they then walk away with a new shared experience.

I decided on the physical design by thinking about what would be the natural form of this experience. I would think that it would be smooth, fun, exciting, and fluid. I also had to think about where I would want it to be placed. I wanted this to be something you had to find, and come across, so I decided I wanted it to be out of the way, somewhere among nature. The ideal spot would have been the Wellington Botanical Gardens, or somewhere similar. I also decided that since this would be more of a “natural” design, I would only use materials I had, or something I could easily reuse.
